# The AI Workflow Diagnostic

### A fixed-scope, evidence-based read on where AI helps in your practice, what it is worth, and in what order to act.

Most AI-readiness work for architecture firms is a slide deck of opinion with a
made-up savings figure on the last page. This is the opposite. It puts numbers
under the conversation: your own timesheet, your own work, measured against a
published framework, with every soft assumption labelled as such.

You are not buying a model that tells you your AI savings. You are buying a
defensible map of where AI helps in your practice, produced from your own data
through a method you can see and challenge.

---

## What you provide

- **A timesheet export.** Hours by person, role, project phase and activity. This
  is what makes the cost base real. We handle the mapping from your phase and role
  names to the framework; messy exports are expected.
- **Salary-cost rates per grade.** Fully-loaded hourly cost, so the figure is your
  real cost base, not charge-out.
- **A half-day workshop** with people who know how the work actually flows, to
  elicit the tool autonomy, the adoption assumptions, and how work moves and loops
  between stages.

## What we do

A fixed engagement of a few weeks, not an open-ended consulting relationship.

1. Classify your work against the framework (the task-by-stage grid).
2. Derive your work groupings from your data rather than imposing them.
3. Cost each stage from your timesheet and grade rates.
4. Map your work onto the 2026 tooling trajectory: where the tools have moved, how
   end-to-end they now reach, and which run on your own data.
5. Run the workshop to elicit the soft inputs.
6. Produce the deliverable and the conversion decision.

## What you get

A single partner-facing report, readable in twenty minutes, with the decision on
the first page:

- **The opportunity map** and the readiness map: where AI helps, and whether the
  tooling is there yet or waiting on your own data.
- **A measured cost base**, by stage, from your timesheet.
- **A banded business case**: capacity released under low, expected and high
  adoption, never a single number.
- **The conversion decision**: released capacity is hours, not money. It books
  value only by running the work with fewer hours (margin) or re-selling the freed
  time as fee (growth). The output is a decision for the partnership, not a promise.
- **A sequenced roadmap**: act now where capable tooling runs on project data,
  watch where the value or the tooling is not there yet, and build foundations
  where the AI needs your own data in order first.
- **A data-readiness recommendation.**

## Why it holds up

The cost base is measured arithmetic on your timesheet. The classification is read
against Phil Bernstein's published framework (*Machine Learning: Architecture in
the Age of AI*, RIBA 2022). The systemic and flow analysis uses Eber's
platform-oriented management. The soft inputs, tool autonomy and adoption, are
elicited in the workshop and labelled as elicited everywhere they appear.

That last point is the value, not a weakness. The structured conversation that
turns scattered intuition into a single defensible map is what you are paying for.
Every method of this kind is built on elicited estimates handled exactly this way.
